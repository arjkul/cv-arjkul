### Gaps & Mitigation Strategy

**Gap 1: Production AI/ML Product Experience (Medium Severity)**

**The Gap:** JD wants "2+ years with AI/ML products" as shipped features. CV shows certifications + tool usage, no production launch.

**Why it's not a blocker:** 
- Arjun has 5+ years PM experience (the heavier signal)
- AI fluency is demonstrated (certs, active Claude/Cursor/N8N usage)
- AI product management ≠ AI engineering — PM is about product sense + experimentation, not coding
- At Senior level, "AI PM" means "product leader who understands AI capabilities and leads teams to build AI features," not "former ML engineer"

**Mitigation Plan:**
1. **Reframe the narrative:** "I have 5+ years of product thinking + 2+ years of hands-on AI tool experience (AWS GenAI, Claude, Cursor, N8N). I haven't shipped a Claude-powered feature in production, but I've shipped multiple zero-to-one products and I speak the language of model evaluation and prompt engineering."
2. **Prepare case study:** Walk through how you'd approach "designing an AI personalization feature for merchants" using your Amazon data modeling work as foundation
3. **Prove fluency:** Mention specific AWS AI certification modules (LLM deployment, prompt engineering); show you can discuss model selection, cost-benefit tradeoffs
4. **Offer learning:** "I'm excited to learn CDP tools (Segment, mParticle) and deepen my model evaluation skills. I learn fast by building — that's how I've always worked."

**Gap 2: CDP Architecture (Medium Severity)**

**The Gap:** JD wants "hands-on CDP architecture experience" (identity resolution, audience segmentation). CV shows data modeling, not CDP-specific tools.

**Why it's not a blocker:**
- CDP is a tool/framework; data architecture is the underlying discipline
- Arjun has designed data collection schemas, forecasting models, segmentation logic (Amazon, ShipMonk)
- Most PM hires learn CDP tools on the job (Segment, mParticle, Lytics are learnable in 2-4 weeks)

**Mitigation Plan:**
1. **Reframe:** "I haven't deployed Segment or mParticle, but I've architected data schemas for real-time decision-making at Amazon — customer segmentation for labor allocation, churn forecasting. The principles are identical: clean data ingestion, identity resolution, fast queries, real-time activation."
2. **Prepare a deep dive:** Walk through your Amazon forecasting model as a CDP analogy: data sources → schema design → identity matching → segmentation → activation (labor allocation)
3. **Offer ramp:** "I'd ramp quickly on CDP tools — they're abstractions over concepts I already understand."

**Gap 3: TCPA/GDPR/CCPA Compliance (Medium Severity)**

**The Gap:** SMS marketing has hard regulatory requirements (TCPA = Telephone Consumer Protection Act for SMS). CV shows general compliance context (HPCL, Amazon) but not SMS-specific.

**Why it's not a blocker:**
- Compliance is learnable in onboarding
- Arjun has worked at Amazon (GDPR, CCPA compliance baked into product) and HPCL (regulatory/environmental compliance mindset)
- This is a "good to have," not "must have" for Day 1

**Mitigation Plan:**
1. **Acknowledge:** "TCPA for SMS is new to me, but I've worked at Amazon where GDPR and CCPA compliance is built into every product decision."
